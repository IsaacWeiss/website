The Medicine Cabinet

December 29, 2021

Already you can see two unforeseen issues:

  1. A vertical electrical wire was stapled to the stud. By the time I took this picture I had already removed the staples and taped the wire to the back of the drywall on the far side; I determined that there was enough slack to just push the cabinet in and trap the wire in between the back of the cabinet and the far side of the cavity.
  2. The drywall was screwed into the stud. Well, of course it would be. On this side, I just cut and tore and ripped the drywall off the screw. But what about the drywall attached to the other side? What was supposed to happen to that when I removed the stud entirely? I could patch the drywall if it got damaged. But, as it happens, this wall separated the bathroom from the kitchen. The other side of this wall was tiled!
